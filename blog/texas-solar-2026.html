The Big One

30% Federal Tax Credit (ITC)

If your solar system costs $30,000, you get $9,000 back from the IRS. It's a dollar-for-dollar credit on your federal taxes. This is valid through 2032.

$9,000 Potential Savings on a $30k System
Texas Exclusive

Property Tax Exemption for Solar

Usually, solar panels increase your property tax bill. Not here. Texas law says the added value of solar panels cannot increase your property tax assessment. You get the home value bump without the extra tax bill.

0% Property Tax Increase

What About Net Metering?

Texas has a unique energy market. Some utilities offer full 1:1 net metering (you get credit for every solar watt you send back). Others offer a lower rate.

Action Step: Check your utility's current net metering policy before signing with an installer.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does solar cost in Texas?

The average system runs between $2.75 to $3.50 per watt. For a standard 10kW home system, expect $27,000–$35,000 before incentives.

Does solar work well in Texas heat?

Absolutely. While extreme heat drops efficiency slightly, Texas has so many peak-sun hours that your yearly total output will be massive.
