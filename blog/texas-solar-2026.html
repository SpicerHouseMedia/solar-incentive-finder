Top Texas Solar Incentives

The Big One

30% Federal Tax Credit (ITC)

This applies to every state, including Texas. If your solar system costs $30,000, you get $9,000 back from the IRS. It's a dollar-for-dollar credit on your federal taxes. This is valid through 2032.

State Specific

Texas Property Tax Exemption for Solar

This is huge for Texas homeowners. Usually, a solar panel upgrade increases your home value, which increases your property tax. Not here. Texas law says the added value of solar panels cannot increase your property tax assessment. You get the home value bump without the extra tax bill.

Utility Programs

City & Utility Rebates

Many Texas cities and power companies offer extra cash back to get on the grid. Common programs include:

  • Austin Energy: Often has specific rebate caps for residential installs.
  • CPS Energy (San Antonio): One of the largest municipal utility solar programs in the US.
  • Oncor: Net metering programs for selling excess power back.

What About Net Metering?

Texas has a unique energy market. Some utilities offer full 1:1 net metering (you get credit for every solar watt you send back). Others offer a lower rate. It depends entirely on your specific utility provider.

Action Step: Check your utility's current net metering policy before signing with an installer.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does solar cost in Texas?

The average system runs between $2.75 to $3.50 per watt. For a standard 10kW home system, expect $27,000–$35,000 before incentives.

Does solar work well in Texas heat?

Absolutely. Panels are tested for extreme conditions. While extreme heat drops efficiency slightly, Texas has so many peak-sun hours that your yearly total output will be massive.
