Virginia is one of the most underrated states for solar power in the U.S. The Old Dominion gets solid sun exposure, has some of the strongest utility-scale solar growth in the country, and offers homeowner incentives that most people don't even know about.

The commonwealth passed the Virginia Clean Economy Act in 2020, which set a path to 100% clean electricity by 2050. Since then, solar adoption has been climbing fast. Yet many Virginia homeowners still don't realize how many incentives are on the table.

Whether you're in Northern Virginia, Richmond, Roanoke, or the Hampton Roads area — here's every solar incentive and program available in VA for 2026.

The Big One

30% Federal Tax Credit (ITC)

This is the single biggest solar incentive available. If your solar system costs $28,000, you get $8,400 back from the IRS. It's a dollar-for-dollar reduction on your federal income taxes owed.

The 30% rate is locked in through 2032 under the Inflation Reduction Act. After that it steps down. Starting sooner means locking in the full amount.

VA Exclusive

VA Property Tax Exemption — 100%

This is one of the best property tax policies for solar in the country. Virginia exempts 100% of the added value from solar installations from your property taxes.

Solar panels increase your home value — usually by about $15,000–$25,000 for a typical system. In most states, that means your property taxes would go up. Not in Virginia. The state law says the added value from solar cannot be taxed. Your home is worth more, but your property tax bill stays the same.

Virginia Net Metering Rules

Net metering lets you sell excess solar power back to the grid for credit on your electricity bill. Virginia has state-regulated net metering for investor-owned utilities. Here's how it works:

  1. Your solar panels produce electricity during sunny hours.
  2. If you produce more than you use, the extra goes to the grid.
  3. Your utility gives you bill credits for that power.
  4. At night or on cloudy days, your credits offset what you pull from the grid.

Dominion Energy Virginia Programs

Dominion Energy is the largest utility in Virginia, serving Northern Virginia, Richmond, Hampton Roads, and most of eastern VA. They offer several solar-related programs:

  • ☀️ SolarShare Community Solar — If you can't install rooftop panels (renter, shaded roof, HOA restrictions), you can subscribe to a shared solar project through Dominion's SolarShare program. You get credits on your electric bill proportional to your share of the solar farm's production.
  • ⚡ Net Metering for Rooftop Solar — Dominion customers with rooftop systems up to 25 kW can net meter at the full retail rate. Excess credits roll over to the next billing period.
  • 🔋 Demand Response & Storage Programs — Dominion runs demand response events where customers with solar-plus-battery systems can earn credits by allowing the utility to draw from their battery during peak hours.
  • 🏢 Green Power Program — Dominion's voluntary green power program lets customers support renewable energy projects throughout Virginia by paying a small premium on their electric bill.

Appalachian Power Programs

If you live in southwestern or western Virginia — areas like Roanoke, Blacksburg, Abingdon, or the Shenandoah Valley — Appalachian Power is likely your utility. Here's what they offer:

Appalachian Power Net Metering

Like Dominion, Appalachian Power offers residential net metering for systems up to 25 kW at the retail electricity rate. Your excess production earns bill credits.

Green Power for Homes

Appalachian Power offers a voluntary green energy program. You can purchase renewable energy certificates (RECs) from wind and solar projects to offset your electricity use.

Solar Energy Programs

Appalachian Power has invested in large-scale solar in its service territory. While direct residential rebates are limited, their net metering and green power programs support rooftop solar adoption in western Virginia.

Solarize Virginia — Group Purchasing

Solarize Virginia is a community solar purchasing program run statewide. Local governments, nonprofits, and community groups run "campaigns" where they negotiate discounted group pricing for residents who join together to install solar.

Here's how it works:

  • • Your community launches a Solarize campaign
  • • A pre-vetted installer is selected with group-rate pricing
  • • Residents who sign up during the campaign save 10–20% off typical install costs
  • • Everyone gets the federal 30% tax credit on top

Check if there's an active Solarize campaign in your area before going through a standard installation.

The Honest Part

No Virginia State Income Tax Credit for Solar

Let's be upfront about this: Virginia does not have a state income tax credit for solar.

Virginia used to offer one — a tax credit of up to $2,000 or 30% of the system cost, depending on which one you claimed. But the state solar tax credit expired in 2017 and has not been renewed.

That said, Virginia still stacks up well thanks to the 100% property tax exemption, strong net metering rules, and the federal 30% tax credit. You don't need a state credit to make solar worthwhile here.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does Virginia have a state tax credit for solar?

No. Virginia's state solar tax credit expired in 2017 and has not been renewed. But you still get the federal 30% tax credit, the 100% property tax exemption, and retail net metering. Combined, these make VA a solid solar market.

How does Virginia's property tax exemption for solar work?

Virginia law provides a 100% property tax exemption for residential solar installations. When panels are added, your home's assessed value goes up — but the added value from solar cannot be taxed. So your property tax bill does not increase because of your solar panels.

What is Dominion Energy Solar in Virginia?

Dominion Energy Virginia runs SolarShare, a community solar program. If you cannot install rooftop panels, you can subscribe to a shared solar farm and receive credits on your electric bill. They also offer full net metering for rooftop solar customers.

Does Virginia have a net metering policy?

Yes. Virginia has state-regulated net metering for investor-owned utilities. Dominion Energy and Appalachian Power both offer retail net metering for residential systems up to 25 kW. Excess production earns bill credits at the full retail electricity rate.

Is solar worth it in Virginia?

Yes. Virginia gets about 4 to 4.5 peak sun hours per day. The 100% property tax exemption is one of the strongest in the country. Combined with the 30% federal tax credit, most Virginia homeowners pay off their solar system in 8 to 11 years and save tens of thousands over the 25+ year lifespan.
