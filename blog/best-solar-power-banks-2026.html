🧠 How to Choose a Solar Power Bank

Capacity (mAh)

For weekend trips, 10,000mAh is enough. For longer trips, 20,000mAh+ is essential.

Panel Count

More panels = faster solar charging. Single panels are rarely sufficient to fully charge the bank.

Durability (IP Rating)

If you're using it outdoors, look for IP66 or better to resist rain and dust.

Output Speed

Ensure it supports fast charging for your specific phone model (e.g., PD or QC 3.0).

❓ FAQ

Do solar power banks actually work?

Yes, but they take a very long time. They are best used as a secondary charging method after you have charged the battery via a wall outlet.

Can a solar power bank charge my laptop?

Most standard ones cannot. You would need a high-capacity power station (like a Goal Zero or Jackery) to charge a laptop.

Are they waterproof?

Many are IP66 rated, which means they resist heavy splashes but should not be submerged in water.
