How to Pick the Right Solar Lights

You don't need to be an engineer, but check these three things before buying:

1. Lumens (Brightness)

Most cheap solar lights are under 50 lumens (dim glow). For real visibility, look for 100-200+ lumens. Security lights should be 500+ lumens.

2. IP Rating (Waterproof)

IP44 means "splash proof." IP65+ means "survives a storm." Aim for IP65 for anything outside.

3. Battery Type

Avoid old Ni-Cd batteries. You want Li-ion (2000mAh+). They hold a charge better and last years longer.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do solar lights work in the winter?

Yes, but they need clear sun. Snow on the panel blocks the charge. We recommend tilting your lights or moving them to a south-facing spot in winter.

How long do the batteries last?

Usually 1-2 years. The good news? The best models (like GIGALUMI) have replaceable batteries you can easily swap out.

Why isn't my new light turning on?

Most have a small tab on the battery to save power during shipping. Pull the tab and leave it in direct sun for 8 hours before judging it.
