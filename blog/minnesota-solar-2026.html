🏛️ 30% Federal Tax Credit

The federal Investment Tax Credit (ITC) gives you a tax credit worth 30% of your total solar installation cost. This covers panels, labor, and equipment.

Example: A $30,000 system = $9,000 tax credit.

⭐ Solar*Rewards (Made in MN Bonus)

Minnesota's Solar*Rewards program is one of the most aggressive in the country. It offers performance-based incentives for the first 10 years of operation. If you use solar equipment manufactured in Minnesota, you get an even higher bonus payment per kWh generated.

  • Standard rate: ~$0.035/kWh (varies by block)
  • Made in MN bonus: Adds extra value ($32/watt incentive for some systems)
  • Community solar: Option to subscribe to community projects if rooftop isn't viable

⚡ Net Metering in Minnesota

Minnesota law requires investor-owned utilities like Xcel Energy to provide 1:1 net metering. This means you get full retail credit for the excess power you send to the grid, a major advantage over the wholesale rate paid in many other states.

  • True-up: Annual true-up occurs in April.
  • Eligibility: Systems up to 1 MW are eligible.
  • Co-ops/Municipals: Many rural co-ops also offer strong net metering or buy-back rates.

🏢 Xcel Energy Programs

Xcel Energy Minnesota is the largest utility in the state. Their Solar*Rewards program is the flagship incentive. They also offer:

  • EV Charging Rebates: Rebates for home EV chargers.
  • Efficiency Rebates: Incentives for energy-efficient appliances and heat pumps.

🏠 Property Tax Exemption

Solar energy systems in Minnesota are 100% exempt from property taxes. Your home value increases with solar, but your property tax bill does not.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Is solar worth it in Minnesota winters?

Yes. Summer production is massive and makes up for winter. The annual balance works out in your favor.

Does the Solar*Rewards program have a limit?

Yes, it operates in "blocks" that fill up and decrease rates over time. Apply early to lock in higher payments.

What is the Made in MN bonus?

If a significant portion of your system (panels/racking) is manufactured in Minnesota, you qualify for a higher per-watt incentive.

How long does installation take?

Physical install takes 1-3 days; permits and utility interconnection take 4-8 weeks.

Does snow affect the panels?

Snow usually slides off. Panels can still produce on cloudy days, just at reduced capacity.
