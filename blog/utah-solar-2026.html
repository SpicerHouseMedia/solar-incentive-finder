🏛️ 30% Federal Tax Credit

The federal Investment Tax Credit (ITC) gives you a tax credit worth 30% of your total solar installation cost. This includes panels, labor, inverters, and permits.

Example: A $25,000 system = $7,500 tax credit.

⚡ Net Metering with Rocky Mountain Power

Rocky Mountain Power (RMP) offers net metering across Utah for residential systems up to 25 kW. When your panels produce more than you use, excess electricity flows back to the grid — and RMP credits you.

  • 1:1 kilowatt-hour credit during most of the year
  • Annual true-up in March — unused credits are purchased at the avoided-cost (wholesale) rate
  • 25 kW max for residential net metering
  • Time-of-Use (TOU) billing can increase summer credits when electricity prices peak

🔋 Rocky Mountain Power Programs

RMP offers incentive programs that make upgrading even more valuable:

  • Wattsmart Residential Incentives: Rebates on efficient appliances and home electrification
  • Home Energy Reports: Compare your usage with similar homes to find savings opportunities
  • Solar Connect: Streamlined interconnection process for new solar installations

🤝 Community Solar (Wattsmart Community Solar)

For Utahns who can't install panels (renters, shaded roofs), RMP's community solar program lets you subscribe to a local solar farm. You receive bill credits proportional to your subscription, typically saving 5-15% on electricity costs.

🏠 Property Tax Exemption

Utah provides a 100% property tax exemption for residential solar energy systems. The added value from your solar installation does not increase your property taxes.

⚠️ State Tax Credit: Expired

Utah previously offered a state tax credit for solar, but it expired in 2017. However, the 30% federal credit (valued at up to $7,500+) more than makes up for it, and Utah's sunshine means faster payback regardless.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

How many solar panels does a typical Utah home need?

An average Utah home uses about 11,000 kWh/year. That requires roughly 20-28 panels (350-400W each) and an 8–10 kW system.

Does net metering work in summer with TOU billing?

Yes — summer production is at its peak during Utah's long sunny days. Under TOU billing, credits are earned when rates are highest.

Can I still get a state tax credit?

Utah's state solar tax credit expired in 2017. However, the 30% federal ITC is still available through 2032.

Do I need a permit to install solar in Utah?

Yes, building and electrical permits are required. Most reputable installers handle this as part of the process.

How long does solar installation take in Utah?

Physical installation is typically 1-3 days. Permits and interconnection can add 4-8 weeks depending on your city and utility.
