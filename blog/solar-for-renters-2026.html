🤝 Community Solar: The Easiest Option

Community solar (also known as solar gardens) allows you to subscribe to a local solar farm. You receive credits on your electricity bill for your share of the power produced.

  • Savings: Usually 10-15% off your power bill.
  • No installation: No panels on your roof.
  • Flexible: Most subscriptions require no long-term commitment.
  • Eligibility: Available in over 20 states and growing fast.

Top States: NY, MN, IL, MA, CO, NJ, VA, and ME.

🏙️ Balcony Solar (Plug-in Panels)

Balcony solar setups (like the Anker SOLIX RS40p) are "plug-and-play." You hang a small panel (usually 300-400W) on your balcony railing and plug it into a regular outlet. It offsets your base load, meaning your meter spins slower and you use less grid power.

Ideal For:

  • Apartment dwellers with a South-facing balcony.
  • Condo owners restricted by HOAs from roof-mounted panels.
  • Portable power: You can take the panels with you when you move.

🔋 Portable Solar Panels

If you have a garden or yard but don't own the property, foldable solar panels (200W+) can power your RV, charge a portable power station, or even run a window AC unit on a long extension cord.

"I use a Jackery 1000 paired with a 200W panel every summer on my rental's patio. It runs my laptop and fridge during weekend outages."

💡 Solar Gadgets for Renters

Even without panels, you can use solar to offset your energy bill:

  • Solar outdoor lights: Path lights, string lights, and wall lights that charge during the day and light up at night.
  • Solar power banks: Keep them on a sunny windowsill; they'll top up your phone for free.
  • Solar ovens: Cook your food using 100% sunlight.

🏘️ How to Pitch Solar to Landlords

Landlords care about property value and costs. If you pitch solar installation to your landlord:

  1. Focus on their ROI: Explain the 30% federal tax credit and how it adds value to the property.
  2. Offer to pay a premium: Offer to pay slightly higher rent if they install solar (it still saves you money on the utility bill).
  3. Use a leasing service: Companies like Sunrun will pay for the installation, reducing the landlord's risk to zero.

❓ FAQ

Is community solar available in my area?

Currently, over 20 states have community solar programs. You can check our calculator to see if your specific zip code is covered.

Can I install panels on a rental?

Not without written permission. However, balcony solar is easy to remove when you move.

Does community solar lock me into a contract?

Some do (usually 1-2 years), but many are month-to-month. Check the specific provider in your state.
